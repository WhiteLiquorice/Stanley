Stripe, Inc. is an Irish-American multinational financial technology company that offers payment processing software and application programming interfaces (APIs) for e-commerce websites and mobile applications. Stripe was founded in 2010 by Irish brothers Patrick and John Collison while they were studying at MIT and Harvard, respectively.

The company's headquarters are located in South San Francisco, California, with major offices in Dublin, New York City, Seattle, and Singapore. As of 2024, the company processes hundreds of billions of dollars in payments annually for millions of businesses across 47 countries.

History [edit]

Founding (2010)

Stripe was founded in Palo Alto, California in 2010 by Patrick Collison and John Collison. The brothers identified a gap in the market: while accepting credit card payments online was theoretically possible, it required working with multiple banks and payment processors, each with complex requirements. They aimed to make it dramatically simpler — reducing what previously took weeks to just a few lines of code.

The company was initially called DevPayments before being renamed Stripe. It received its first investment from Y Combinator and was publicly launched in September 2011 after 18 months in private beta. Peter Thiel, Elon Musk, and Sequoia Capital were among the early investors.[2]

Growth and expansion (2011–2020)

By 2012, Stripe had expanded internationally, launching in Canada and the United Kingdom. The company continued to expand its geographic footprint throughout the 2010s, reaching Europe, Asia-Pacific, and Latin American markets. In 2014, Stripe launched Atlas, a program that helps startups around the world incorporate in the United States and access U.S. banking and financial infrastructure.

Funding and valuation [edit]

Stripe has been one of the most highly valued private technology companies in the world. In March 2021, the company raised $600 million at a valuation of $95 billion, making it the most valuable private company in Silicon Valley at the time. Following broader market corrections in 2022, the company reduced its internal valuation to approximately $74 billion.

In 2023, Stripe raised $6.5 billion in a secondary share sale at a valuation of $50 billion — later revised to $65 billion after strong revenue performance. As of 2024, the company has raised over $9 billion in total funding across multiple rounds.
